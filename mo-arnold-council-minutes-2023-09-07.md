# September 7, 2023

Mayor Ron Counts called the meeting to order at 7:00 p.m.

The council meeting was also presented live via Zoom Video Conferencing.

The pledge of allegiance was recited. Councilman Jason Fulbright offered the prayer.

Those present per roll call taken by City Clerk Tammi Casey: Mayor Ron Counts, Mullins, Fulbright, McArthur, Cooley, Plunk (excused), Moritz, Hood (arrived 7:21), Fleischmann (excused), Richison, Bookless, Lehmann, Allison Sweeney, Crutchley, Wagner, Kroupa, and Chief Carroll.

## MOTIONS

Tammi Casey informed council that the Liquor Committee had nothing to bring forward this evening.  

### B. A MOTION TO HOLD A CLOSED SESSION IMMEDIATELY FOLLOWING THE CITY COUNCIL MEETING FOR THE PURPOSE OF DISCUSSING LITIGATION AND REAL ESTATE PURSUANT TO RSMo SECTION 610.021 (1) AND (2)

Mark Hood arrived, via Zoom, at 7:21 p.m.  

## REPORTS FROM MAYOR AND COUNCIL

Mayor Counts — Informed everyone the Mayors Prayer Breakfast will be held this Saturday as well as the Apple Festival at the Farmers Market.  

## ADMINISTRATIVE REPORTS

Bryan Richison — Thanked Bill Lehmann for his hard work with the annual budget and financial audit.  

Chief Carroll — Informed everyone that the police department raised $3,000 for the Missouri Special Olympics during their fundraising event.  

Bill Lehmann — Provided council with the annual financial audit report for fiscal year 2022. The City once again received a clean opinion.  

Judy Wagner — Informed council that she received the new design for the Church Road retention wall. Once approved, they will begin fabrication of the blocks, which will take three to four weeks to complete. Ms. Wagner also informed council that letters were sent to residents regarding the Muddy Creek Watershed project.  

Dave Crutchley — Informed everyone the Apple Festival judging starts at 10:00 a.m. this Saturday. The Rec Center will hold a brief moment of silence on 9/11. Mr. Crutchley also encouraged everyone to attend Arnold Days, which will be held next weekend.  

Mayor Counts announced a five-minute recess before going into Closed Session.  

---

Closed Session ended at 8:23 p.m.  

A motion to adjourn the meeting was made by Butch Cooley. Seconded by Rodney Mullins.

Voice vote: All Yeas.

Meeting adjourned at 8:23 p.m.
