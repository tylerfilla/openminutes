            PUBLIC HEARING FOR CASE 2023-CPD-0l:                  Consideration of amendments to the City of Fenton
            Comprehensive Development Plan.

            Community Development Director Amy Starck gave a presentation summarizing the proposed changes to
            the Comprehensive Plan (The Plan) and       provided a draft of the   proposed changes based on workshop
            discussions held over the past year. A copy of the PowerPoint presentation is attached for the record and
            copies of corresponding     documents    provided to   the Commission     are on file with   the Community
            Development Department.

            Director Starck explained that the format of the Comprehensive Plan is proposed away from the "road
            map"   version of the    1997 Comprehensive      Plan to an   easier booklet   format. She   summarized    the
            Introduction section of the   proposed   Comprehensive    Plan that is divided   into an Overview, detailing
            relevant history and demographics of the City, and Purpose, providing overall objectives of the Plan to act
            as a guide for future development/redevelopment. The Plan is divided into six Planning Areas. Director
            Starck emphasized that special attention was paid to retaining the overall intent and directives of the 1997
            Comprehensive Plan.

            Director Starck   briefly summarized     the six   planning areas;   Public and   Governmental;    Parks and
            Recreation; Residential;   Olde Towne;    Commercial;    and Business   and Industrial. Each   Planning Area
            provides an Overview, Goals, and Objectives and any notable changes from the 1997 Plan are detailed in
            the footnotes. She mentioned that, at the direction of the Commission, the Plan identifies large lots within
            the Residential   Planning Area    as those   with a   minimum    of one   acre in   keeping with   the 1997
            Comprehensive Plan. She further remarked that, based on multiple discussions during the workshops, the
            Commission sought to clarify The Plan and added language to include specifically prohibiting residential
            uses/developments    in the Commercial    Planning area as   they did not   feel it was in keeping   with the
            character and nature of the Planning Area. Based on the proposed changes, Director Starck commented
            that Staff will be bringing needed Zoning Code amendments and rezoning requests to the Commission
            throughout the year.

            Following the presentation, there were no comments from the public or the Commission. Director Starck
            stated that a resolution to adopt the changes to The Plan would be considered at the December 5, 2023
            Planning and Zoning Commission meeting to follow the Public Hearing at 7:00 PM.

   ADJOURNMENT:           6:26 p.m.

            was unanimously approved by voice vote.

To the City of Fenton Planning and Zoning Commission:

I regret that I cannot be there tonight for the approval of the Comprehensive Development Plan. The
updates to the plan represent an important moment for the City, and I am very grateful to my fellow
Commissioners for the thoughtful discussion and debate that contributed to the final updates.
Throughout the workshops, I was impressed with the thorough consideration given to each planning
area, and the Commission's willingness to engage in discussion for the betterment of our City. I fully
support the final plan, and I apologize for missing the vote and public hearing. I had a family conflict that
